Appendix: Recommended Readings

In No White Coat Necessary, I deliberately focused on big-picture concepts.  If, after reading it, you’re hungry to learn more (as I hope you are!), here is a list of recommended readings, as well as a few videos, that correspond to each chapter.  Please note that this is not a bibliography.  Many of the sources I consulted while writing the book (including most of the scientific articles) unfortunately aren’t available for free to the public without connections to a university.  The following list includes a few such sources, but primarily contains those that are available for free to the public.  The links for these sources are correct as of May 2016; I will continue to update this page periodically.  Dates listed refer to when these sources were published.

  • “Benefits and Risks of Caffeine and Caffeinated Beverages” (UpToDate, the leading evidence-based clinical decision support resource; 2016)
  • “Caffeine and Adenosine” (Journal of Alzheimer’s Disease; 2010)
  • “Americans’ Coffee Consumption Is Steady, Few Want to Cut Back” (Gallup Poll; 2015)
  • “Caffeine as an Analgesic Adjuvant for Acute Pain in Adults” (The Cochrane Collaboration; 2014)

  • “Overview of Neuron Function” (Khan Academy; 2013)
  • “Mind-Body Interactions” (Stanford School of Medicine; 2010)
